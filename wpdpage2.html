Historical Places

Home to the Taj Mahal and the Red Fort,
India's historic sites are some of the most iconic and instantly recognisable in the world. Here's our pick of 5 that make for essential visiting for any history buff.


1) Taj Mahal - The crown of the palace

In its harmonious proportions and its fluid incorporation of decorative elements, the Taj Mahal is distinguished as the finest example of Mughal architecture,
a blend of Indian, Persian, and Islamic styles. Other attractions include twin mosque buildings (placed symmetrically on either side of the mausoleum), lovely gardens, and a museum. Foreign tourists have to pay 1100 INR + 200 INR.


2) Red Fort - Lal Qila

The fort’s massive red sandstone walls, enclose a complex of palaces and entertainment halls, projecting balconies,
baths and indoor canals, and geometrical gardens, as well as an ornate mosque. Entry fee for Indians is Rs 10 and for foreign nationals it is Rs 250.


3) Hawa Mahal - the chef-d'œuvre of Maharaja Jai Singh

The monument stands upright because of its pyramidal shape. Its distinctive rosy colour, which is because of the natural sandstone,
is the reason behind Jaipur getting its nickname, .i.e., the Pink City.


4) Amber Palace - BEAUTIFUL MELANGE OF ARCHITECTURE

One of the top tourist attractions of Jaipur, the huge Amer Palace Fort sits atop a small hill, and is located at a distance about 11 km from the main city.
The magnificent Amer Fort is an extensive palace complex that has been built with pale yellow and pink sandstone, and with white marble.


5) Qutub Minar - 'Might of Islam'

The Qutub Minar is a five-storeyed red sandstone tower built by Muslim conquerors in the thirteenth century to commemorate their final triumph over the Rajput rulers of Delhi (Qutub means victory), while also serving as a tower from where muezzins
(criers) call for prayer at the Quwwatu'l-Islam mosque nearby.
